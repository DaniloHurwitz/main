How I Started in Freelancing: From Zero to $1,000/Month While Studying Medicine

Starting as a freelancer can feel overwhelming, especially when you’re balancing a full-time university schedule. Back in May 2023, I found myself wanting to make money while studying medicine. I knew I couldn’t dedicate full days to a job, but I still wanted to earn something meaningful without compromising my studies.

Finding My Niche

The first step was identifying what I could offer. I realized that I had skills in translation, proofreading, and video editing, all in multiple languages—Hebrew, English, and Spanish. I started small, offering my services at very low rates just to build credibility and get my first clients. At first, I was nervous and unsure if anyone would hire me, but I quickly learned that starting with a reasonable price is better than waiting for the “perfect” opportunity.

Lessons Learned Early On

Once I got my first few projects, I realized two things:

  • Niche matters. I focused on technical translations, proofreading for websites, and editing professional videos. By specializing, I could stand out among general freelancers.
  • Reputation is everything. Even small jobs completed perfectly led to 5-star reviews, which immediately attracted more clients willing to pay higher rates.

Growing My Freelance Income

Over time, I began raising my rates gradually, matching the quality and experience I was gaining. By working smart—taking projects I could handle efficiently—I reached a point where I was earning over $1,000 per month, all while studying medicine full-time.

Practical Tips for Beginners

  • Start with what you know. Even if it’s simple, offering a clear, valuable service helps you get your first clients.
  • Don’t undervalue your work forever. Low rates are okay to start, but your skills deserve proper compensation.
  • Focus on quality. Clients remember excellent work, and reviews are gold for freelancing growth.
  • Be consistent. Even a few hours per week can accumulate into a solid income if you plan projects wisely.
  • Build your profile carefully. Showcase past work, language skills, and professionalism.

Final Thoughts

Freelancing taught me not only how to earn money on the side but also how to manage time, communicate professionally, and value my own skills. It’s a journey of growth as much as it is a source of income.

If you’re a student like me, trying to make money while juggling classes and exams, starting as a freelancer is absolutely possible. The key is to start small, stay consistent, and build credibility. From my first $5 project to making $1,000 a month, every step was a learning opportunity—and one that can be repeated by anyone willing to put in the effort.
