The Challenge

The client, a HealthTech startup, needed to translate their patient-facing Informed Consent Form (ICF) from Hebrew to English for an upcoming international clinical trial. The translation needed to be 100% accurate, culturally sensitive, and use precise clinical terminology to ensure full patient comprehension and meet strict IRB (Institutional Review Board) compliance.

The Solution

Leveraging my background as a medical student, I performed a multi-stage translation process. This involved:

  1. Initial translation focusing on semantic accuracy.
  2. Rigorous terminology research to align with FDA/EMA guidelines.
  3. A "cultural nuance" pass to ensure the tone was reassuring and clear for patients, not just technical.
  4. A final QA pass to check for consistency and formatting.

The Result

The final translated ICF was delivered two days ahead of schedule. The client reported that the document passed their legal and regulatory review with zero terminology errors, allowing them to proceed with their IRB submission without delay.
